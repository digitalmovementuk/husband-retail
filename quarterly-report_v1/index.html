Chapter 01

Luxury macro overview

Headline performance is stable — but underneath, the market is reshuffling. Here's the size of the prize, where growth is moving, and what 2026 could hold.

€0B Total global luxury market, 2025EUp +12/14% on 2019, easing −3/−1% on 2024 at current FX. Source: Bain & Company.

Stable on the surface, shifting underneath

Personal luxury goods remain the largest single category, yet they are dipping — while hospitality, gourmet food, private jets and yachts power ahead.

  • Personal luxury goods stay the biggest category at €358B, but are softening slightly.
  • Luxury cars, fine wines & spirits and design furniture hold steady, giving the market its ballast.
  • Private jets & yachts keep thriving on demand for space, comfort and lower emissions.
  • Experiences — sport (F1, tennis, golf), theme parks, safaris and eno-gastronomy — blend leisure, status and exclusivity.
  • Luxury food is on a robust growth path, led by hubs in the Middle East and Southeast Asia.

Luxury · Travel RetailJan 2026

LVMH's DFS sells Hong Kong & Macau stores, exits key Hainan project

LVMH is selling DFS travel-retail operations in Hong Kong and Macau to China Tourism Group Duty Free for $395M while keeping the brand, and scrapping its Yalong Bay project in Hainan. It follows closing Guam after 50 years and exiting Hawaii after 63 years (its Waikiki store and Honolulu Airport outlet close in March), plus T-Galleria withdrawals in New Zealand, Australia and Saipan, Venice's Fondaco dei Tedeschi, and moving Paris's La Samaritaine into a new governance structure.

Real EstateJan 2026

New World close to deal giving Blackstone a controlling stake

Embattled Hong Kong developer New World — about 45% held by the Cheng family — is reportedly near an agreement making Blackstone its largest shareholder, enabling debt restructuring after heavy spend on the SkyCity entertainment hub and Kai Tak Sports Park. Potential asset sales could include the Rosewood hotel group and its landmark K11 developments in Hangzhou, Shenzhen and Shanghai.

For operators & owners

Where to point the mix

  • Lean into what's growing. Experiences, F&B and outlet formats are gaining share, while monobrand and department-store space sits under scrutiny — reweight the mix accordingly.
  • Court the Gen-Z brands. Gen Z's share has jumped from 8% to 19% — the labels that genuinely resonate with them are the footfall you're underwriting.
  • Follow the demand. Growth is moving to the Middle East, Southeast Asia and Mexico — point expansion and tenant sourcing there.
Chapter 02

The future of experience

Retail is becoming a paradise for collaboration — brands with developers, architects with artists. Seven themes are putting experience at the forefront of the world's most exciting destinations. Tap to expand each.

0 Experience themes redefining the storeFrom a sense of place and social theatre to joy commerce and the AI dreamscape.
01A sense of placeThe "third space" beyond home and work

The "third space" gives shoppers a setting for social interaction, relaxation and shopping — welcoming atmospheres where community can flourish. From Parisian icons to London's grandes dames, department stores have filled themselves with lounges, libraries and beauty halls.

KaDeWe Berlin tackled its 60,000m² floorplate by splitting into four architectural "quadrants" linked by "primary streets" — an internal urban grid that optimises navigation and flow.

In focus
La Samaritaine · Paris
KaDeWe · Berlin — four quadrants
Harrods & Selfridges · London
Takashimaya · Japan
SKP · Chengdu
02Retail as social theatreAtriums as stages for art, pop-ups and F&B

Innovative programming turns the atrium into a platform for immersive installations, contemporary art and ever-changing pop-ups — adding cultural significance, not just "Instagrammable moments." Le Bon Marché, the world's first true department store, in January 2026 welcomed Chinese artist Song Dong with the exhibition "Miscellaneous and Varied Objects – 百货 (bǎihuò)"; Song Dong has exhibited at major museums including MOMA.

Food & beverage has shifted from amenity to revenue driver — F&B space in shopping centres has more than doubled in a decade, reaching up to 20% of leasing in some malls.

In focus
Le Bon Marché · Paris — Song Dong
Harrods Food Hall · London
03Back to service70%+ of customers still want human interaction

Per PwC, more than 70% of global customers want human interaction while shopping. Service today is operational excellence — staff knowledge, checkout efficiency, cleanliness, and ease of arrival, all enabled by people and systems.

Le Printemps New York layers in tailoring, wardrobe consultations, spa, fragrance profiling and personal shopping. Marina Bay Sands blends retail and hospitality via a single digital platform. The retailers that get it — Hermès, Apple, Restoration Hardware, Le Bon Marché — are thriving.

In focus
Le Printemps · New York
The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands · Singapore
Restoration Hardware · Houston
04Hyper-hybrid retailCommerce, culture and service intentionally blurred

Hyper-Hybrid Retail takes the lessons of Le Bon Marché and makes them dynamic and immersive. Emsphere Bangkok reimagines the mall as a vertical village of interconnected, themed floors under one raw industrial aesthetic.

Cross-sector collaborations multiply: product drops in grocery stores (Alo Yoga × Erewhon), rotating retail in hotel lobbies, furniture brands entering fashion. Harvey Nichols is reworking its London ground floor into a "Living Magazine."

In focus
Emsphere · Bangkok — vertical village
Alo Yoga × Erewhon · Los Angeles
Harvey Nichols · London — Living Magazine
05Culturally chargedWhen the place itself transcends the product

Retail is no longer satisfied with capturing attention — it earns it through culturally relevant, architecturally impactful, emotionally engaging environments. To Summer, a modern Chinese fragrance house, chooses a Qing-dynasty courtyard in Beijing or a villa on Shanghai's Hunan Road — sanctuaries more than stores.

ICONSIAM Bangkok blends ultra-luxury retail with cultural immersion: Thai craftsmanship in modern architecture, regional culinary zones, and an outdoor park woven into the experience.

In focus
To Summer · Beijing & Shanghai
ICONSIAM · Bangkok — riverside
06Joy commerceShopping as a dopamine-fuelled emotional escape

"Joy Commerce" positions shopping as an emotional escape — gamified, high-touch design that turns stores into dopamine-fuelled destinations offering inspiration and comfort.

It's surfacing in surprising niches: Jellycat's Café in Shanghai brings plush-toy whimsy to themed desserts; Selfridges hosts a UK fish-and-chips experience; and Nanjing's Deji Plaza turned its lavishly themed restrooms into social-media sensations — spatial experimentation over lease efficiency.

In focus
Jellycat Café · Shanghai
Deji Plaza washrooms · Nanjing
07AI & the omni-channel dreamscapeSuspension of disbelief, inside the store

AI will breathe new life into physical brand environments, deepening connections to imaginative brand narratives — enchanting moments of suspension of disbelief where people step into alternative worlds.

Xydrobe at Harrods, built with Vacheron Constantin, is a plug-in immersive VR cinema synchronising scent, sound and temperature to a visual storyline. Future Stores offers a stage for holographic campaigns and AI-assisted consultations.

In focus
Xydrobe × Vacheron Constantin · Harrods, London
Future Stores · London
For operators & owners

Programme the asset, don't just lease it

  • Budget for placemaking. The atrium is a stage and the "third space" is the draw — cultural programming is now a footfall driver, not a nice-to-have.
  • Plan for F&B. Food & beverage has more than doubled in a decade, taking up to 20% of leasing in some malls — design the space and the covenants for it.
  • Compete on service. 70%+ of shoppers still want human interaction; staffing, ease of arrival and dwell-time amenities are what set the destination apart.
The future of retail will transcend disciplines and geographies — driven by a shared vision to inspire and engage people in meaningful ways.
